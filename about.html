1,500 HP V8 CRATE ENGINE. ENGINE.

Horsepower has never been more accessible than it is today. Walk into your local Toyota dealership and you can buy a Camry that has more power under the hood than a Ferrari 348 ever had. There are muscle cars for sale right now that rival the power outputs of the world’s fastest supercars, that also happen to cost less than six-figures. Automakers even sell an extensive number of crate motors with a wide range of power outputs, giving gearheads a chance to bring some serious performance to their project cars. But what if you are someone who isn’t satisfied by even the most powerful offerings coming from said automakers? We’d like to introduce you to Nelson Racing Engines and their new 427 cubic-inch twin-turbo V8 crate engine.

Oh yeah, it's that good. See for yourself.

The engine goes in a bracket racing dragster where Jason has already been a consistent winner at high dollar bracket races with a 12oo horsepower big block Chevy. The goal here is to achieve a 4.20 second or better eighth mile with a calmer, more consistent car for extended usage. The big block generated 0.925 sixty foot times but it was almost too aggressive off the starting line. The new car uses a Powerglide and a 7000 stall converter. The management system is setup to take out timing in low gear to calm the chassis down and then feed the timing back in as boost rises and the methanol fuel turns on. A more powerful, but calmer car should generate more consistency and thus more wins and a longer service life for the engine.
